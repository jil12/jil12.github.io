Project 1: TXI Launch Pad
Project Hero: Looping GIF or Video of the interactive cornhole game in action

At a Glance
Role: Project Manager and Lead Raspberry Pi Developer
Timeline: 2 Months
Team: Multidisciplinary
Tools: Python, Raspberry Pi, Hardware Wiring, Notion, Figma, Miro

Project Overview
I directed the software development and physical fabrication of an interactive smart-game installation called TXI Launch Pad. I managed the hardware logic and team coordination to ensure a reliable physical play experience.

The Challenge
The main goal was bridging the gap between digital software and physical hardware. The Raspberry Pi presented severe technical constraints including audio clipping, frame rate drops, and video transition latency. Physically, the internal wiring constantly disconnected during the game movement before we could secure the components.

The Approach
I engineered the core interactive logic and media playback triggers using Python. I optimized the code to bypass the Raspberry Pi limitations. I also co-led the physical assembly and wiring. To solve the disconnect issues, we implemented a rigorous testing loop. We debugged the wiring during physical movement tests before finalizing the soldering and fixed-position assembly.

The Impact
We successfully launched a durable, interactive physical game installation. The installation seamlessly bridged immersive digital animation with tactile, real-world user interactions.

Learnings and Takeaways
Physical prototyping requires a completely different cadence than software development. Building early, small-scale physical prototypes is a necessity. Testing the hardware sooner helped us surface fabrication bugs and wiring issues before the final build. This process saved crucial development time.

Project 2: EZ Line Inclusive Beauty
Project Hero: High-res photo or 3D render of the final EZ Line physical prototype

At a Glance
Role: Product Designer and Researcher
Timeline: 3 Months
Team: 5 UX Designers and Researchers
Tools: Autodesk Fusion, 3D Printing, Figma

Project Overview
I led the UX research and physical prototyping of an inclusive makeup tool for blind and low-vision users. I developed a validated go-to-market strategy forecasting a profitable business model.

The Challenge
Translating empathetic user research into a functional, manufacturable physical product required a clear vision. We needed to address unmet needs in makeup accessibility through cost-effective and reusable solutions. I also had to learn 3D modeling and printing from scratch under a strict three-month deadline.

The Approach
Our team conducted over 23 hours of direct observation and testing with more than 15 participants. We analyzed competitor products via perceptual mapping to identify gaps in cost-effectiveness. The team sketched over 100 concepts to find the right direction. I then rapidly upskilled in Autodesk Fusion to create two functional 3D models. We printed these models to conduct three distinct iterations of real-world tactile user testing. I developed a robust business case and revenue model for a stakeholder pitch. This case outlined specific business-to-consumer e-commerce and retail partnership channels.

The Impact
We delivered a high-fidelity physical prototype proven to be accessible to the target demographic. We forecasted a $3.50 profit per unit and an 8% annual growth trajectory targeting a $7B U.S. cosmetic tools segment.

Learnings and Takeaways
Learning new technical skills like 3D printing becomes approachable when driven by a clear user need. Cross-functional success relies on identifying and maximizing the unique strengths of every team member.

Project 3: Multi-Sensory Haptic Demo App
Project Hero: Video or high-res image of the haptic audiobook app in use

At a Glance
Role: Product Designer and Prototyper
Timeline: 1 Month
Team: Startup Team
Tools: Avalonia UI, Figma, Agile Methodologies

Project Overview
I designed and prototyped a haptic audiobook app to create an intuitive multi-sensory storytelling experience. The project originated from a hackathon where our team won first place out of ten teams.

The Challenge
We needed to synthesize market research into actionable design briefs and storytelling assets for stakeholders. The goal was to deliver a customizable prototype rapidly to adapt to different audiences and enterprise client needs. We also needed an intuitive handoff process so non-technical stakeholders could confidently present the demo.

The Approach
I mapped out the core requirements and wrote the business model canvas to define the product strategy. I built the front-end using Avalonia UI and facilitated rapid iteration using Agile practices. This approach allowed us to deliver a functional prototype within a one-month sprint. For the handoff, I directly trained the leadership on how to operate and present the prototype for immediate marketing use.

The Impact
We secured interest from three or more potential enterprise clients. The successful reception of this demo secured internal confirmation to pivot the storytelling technology into a new consumer product line.

Learnings and Takeaways
Direct stakeholder handoffs require clear communication and intuitive design. Building a prototype is only half the job. Ensuring the team can easily use the prototype to tell a compelling story is what actually drives business value.
